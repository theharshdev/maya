What is Maya?

A central concept in Hindu philosophy and translated as illusion or ignorance.

A common example used to explain Maya is mistaking a rope for a snake in dim light. The fear experienced is real, even though the snake does not actually exist. When proper light appears, the illusion disappears and the rope is seen as it truly is. In the same way, the world appears real to us, but this reality is shaped by ignorance rather than true knowledge.

Brahman is the ultimate, unchanging reality, and Atman is the true self within each individual.

As long as one remains under the influence of Maya, lasting peace and freedom cannot be achieved.

Hindu philosophy teaches that liberation does not require escaping the world but understanding it correctly.

Maya deeply affects human identity. Under its influence, individuals identify themselves with their physical body, thoughts, emotions, and social roles.

Pleasure and pain, success and failure, gain and loss are experienced intensely because they are mistaken for permanent realities.

Jnana Yoga emphasizes self-inquiry and knowledge, helping individuals discriminate between the real and the unreal.
